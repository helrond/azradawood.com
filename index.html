Azra Dawood

I am a historian, architect, educator, and curator. My work focuses on built environments and artistic practices, studying these within both hyperlocal and global contexts. I am particularly drawn to issues related to diaspora and immigration, internationalism, critical perspectives on philanthropy, and religion. I completed my PhD in 2018 from MIT's Aga Khan Program for Islamic Architecture and the History, Theory and Criticism of Architecture and Art program.

My teaching, research/writing, architectural, and curatorial/public-history careers have spanned Austin, Cambridge (MA), Houston, Karachi, and New York City. For an overview of my work, see my CV.
